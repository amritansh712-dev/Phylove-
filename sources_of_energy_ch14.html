Section 1

What Makes an Ideal Energy Source?

NCERT defines four essential criteria that every good, efficient energy source must satisfy. No single source fulfills all four perfectly — which is why we need a diverse energy mix.

bolt

High Calorific Output

Must perform a large amount of work per unit volume or mass. Higher calorific value = more energy released per kg of fuel burned.

inventory_2

Easy to Store & Transport

Must be easy to store and transport safely without excessive leakage risks. Liquid fuels score high; hydrogen gas scores low here.

schedule

Easily Accessible

Should be easily accessible over long durations. Renewables like solar and wind far outperform finite fossil fuels here.

eco

Economical & Low Pollution

Should be highly economical and produce minimal environmental pollution. Fossil fuels score the lowest; solar and wind score highest.

Section 2

Conventional Energy Sources

Traditional sources used for centuries. Well-established but most are non-renewable and cause significant environmental damage.

Fossil Fuels

Coal and petroleum are non-renewable energy reserves formed over millions of years under extreme subterranean heat and pressure from ancient buried biomass.

⚠ Environmental Impact

  • • Combustion releases CO, CO₂, NOₓ, and SO₂
  • • CO₂ causes the greenhouse effect & global warming
  • • SO₂ and NOₓ react with rainwater → Acid Rain
  • • Particulate smoke causes respiratory diseases

Formation (Remember for Exams)

Ancient forests & marine organisms were buried under sediments. Over millions of years, heat + pressure transformed them → coal (from plants) and petroleum/natural gas (from marine organisms).

Why We Must Conserve

Known petroleum reserves may last only ~40–50 years. Coal reserves ~200 years. These took millions of years to form but are being depleted in centuries.

Greenhouse Effect

CO₂ traps outgoing infrared radiation, warming the Earth

Thermal Power Plants

In thermal power plants, massive volumes of fossil fuels are burnt daily to heat water, produce high-pressure steam, which drives large turbines to generate electricity.

Working Steps

1Coal is burned in a furnace
2Heat converts water into high-pressure steam in a boiler
3Steam drives turbine blades at high speed
4Turbine rotates generator coils → electricity
5Transmitted via high-voltage power lines

Key Exam Point

Transmission of electricity is more efficient than transporting raw coal or petroleum over identical distances. This is why power plants are built near coal mines.

Limitation: Uses finite fossil fuels; releases CO₂, SO₂, and NOₓ; thermal pollution of nearby water bodies.

Hydroelectric Power Plants

Converts the potential energy of water at heights and kinetic energy of flowing water into electrical energy. Clean and renewable.

Working Mechanism

  • • Large dams built across rivers trap water at height → Potential Energy (PE = mgh)
  • • Released water converts PE to kinetic energy
  • • Flowing water turns turbine blades → generator → electricity
  • • No combustion, no emissions — clean energy

⚠ Three Major Drawbacks (NCERT Important)

Social: Displaces vast numbers of local peasants and tribals without fair compensation
Economic: Consumes enormous public funds without proportionate benefits
Environmental: Massive deforestation, loss of biodiversity, permanent ecological imbalance

Biomass & Biogas

Biomass = organic material from plants or animal waste (wood, agricultural waste, cow dung). India has a vast biomass resource.

Traditional Use — Inefficient

Burning biomass directly is highly inefficient, produces excessive harmful smoke and carbon particles, causing indoor air pollution and respiratory diseases.

Modern Use: Biogas Plant

Cow dung, plant waste, & sewage undergo anaerobic digestion (decomposition without oxygen) inside a biogas plant.

Inputs:Cow dung + water + plant waste
Output gas:~75% CH₄ + CO₂ + trace gases
Slurry residue:Rich in N & P → excellent manure

✓ Advantages of Biogas

  • ✓ Burns cleanly — no smoke, no residue
  • ✓ Slurry is rich manure for agriculture
  • ✓ Reduces dependence on LPG/kerosene
  • ✓ Manages cow dung waste hygienically
  • ✓ Renewable — can be produced continuously

Biogas Composition

CH₄ 75%
CO₂ 20%
Other 5%

Wind Energy

The kinetic energy of wind is harnessed via large windmills to turn a generator shaft and produce electricity. Multiple windmills over a large area = wind energy farm.

Critical Requirement

Wind speed must be above 15 km/h and must blow consistently throughout the year for commercial generation.

✓ Advantages

  • ✓ Clean and renewable — zero fuel cost
  • ✓ No pollution, no CO₂ emissions
  • ✓ Land between windmills can still be used for farming

✗ Disadvantages

  • ✗ Requires high initial capital investment
  • ✗ Needs very large land area
  • ✗ Location-dependent — only specific geographic zones
  • ✗ Wind must blow consistently (≥15 km/h)
  • ✗ Intermittent — cannot generate 24/7

Power Formula

P = ½ρAv³

Power is proportional to the cube of wind speed. Doubling wind speed → 8× more power!

Section 3

Non-Conventional Energy Sources

Alternative, mostly renewable technologies offering cleaner substitutes to fossil fuels. These represent the future of sustainable energy.

light_mode

Solar Energy

Renewable · Non-Conventional

Solar energy is harnessed using solar cookers or solar cells (photovoltaic cells).

Solar Cells
  • • Made of semiconducting silicon
  • • Convert solar energy directly into DC electricity
  • • Multiple cells → solar panel
  • • Multiple panels → solar array / solar plant
  • • Zero maintenance, no moving parts
  • • Work efficiently without focusing devices
  • • Ideal for remote regions with no grid access

Nuclear Energy

Non-Renewable · Non-Conventional

Harnesses enormous energy released during nuclear fission of heavy atoms like U-235, Pu-239, or Th-232.

Nuclear Fission Steps
①Heavy nucleus (U-235) bombarded with low-energy neutrons
②Nucleus splits into two lighter nuclei
③Releases massive energy + 2–3 neutrons
④New neutrons trigger more fissions → chain reaction
Chain Reaction Simulator — Click U-235 to trigger
U-235 + n → Ba-141 + Kr-92 + 3n + 200 MeV energy
⚠ Serious Risks
  • • Radioactive waste is toxic for thousands of years
  • • Risk of accidental leakage (Chernobyl 1986, Fukushima 2011)
  • • High construction & maintenance costs
  • • Nuclear material can be misused for weapons
water

Ocean Energy Systems

Three distinct types
1. Tidal Energy

Driven by the gravitational pull of the Moon, tides rise and fall daily. A dam across a narrow sea opening traps the tidal flow to drive turbines.

2. Wave Energy

Kinetic energy of strong oceanic surface waves trapped near shorelines is used to drive generators via the oscillating motion.

3. Ocean Thermal Energy (OTE)

Operates on the temperature difference (~20°C) between warm surface water and cold deep sea water. This gradient boils volatile ammonia (NH₃), whose vapors drive turbines.

Geothermal Energy

Renewable · Non-Conventional

Structural changes in Earth's crust force hot molten rocks (magma) upward into regions called hot spots.

Working Mechanism
  • • Underground water contacts geothermal hot spots
  • • Water turns into high-pressure steam
  • • Steam is channeled through drilled pipes to surface
  • • Steam drives generator turbines → electricity
  • • Available 24/7 regardless of weather — unlike solar/wind
Key Advantage: Available 24/7 all year round — far more reliable than solar or wind which are intermittent.
